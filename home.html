Rituals in sindhi wedding

Pakki Mishri

Pakki Mishri is the formal engagement ceremony. Rings are exchanged between the bride and the groom.

Dev Bithana

Lord Ganesha and other sacred idols are placed on a wooden stool called 'Chakki'. It is believed that God will take care of any hurdle that might trouble the wedding.

Lada (Sangeet)

It is the Sindhi Ladies Sangeet. The name is after the traditional songs that are sung along with dholak in high pitch by the ladies of the house and the neighborhood.

Rituals in sindhi wedding

Tih

A day prior to the wedding, some 'Shubh Samagri' like rice, coconut, etc. is taken by the bride's guardian to groom's place and a 'puja' is conducted.

Mehndi

Mehndi is part of the Solah Shringar, or sixteen bridal adornments, which is a ritual for the beautification of the bride from head to toe at the time of their wedding.

Haldi ceremony

A paste of haldi is applied on the bride and the groom's body before their wedding day. The colour yellow is also considered auspicious, and thus is said to ward off the evil.

Rituals in sindhi wedding

Baraat

The groom's wedding procession to the bride's house.It refers to welcome of the family of the groom by the bride's family.

Jai Mala

The jaimala is a token of acceptance. When the bride and the groom exchange the garland, it symbolises the acceptance of would-be man by the would-be wife as her partner for life.

Four Pheras

The circling around fire is called phera. This involves chanting of holy verses and taking of wedding vows.

Rituals in sindhi wedding

Vidai

It is a form of a farewell ceremony organized by the family of the bride to officially bid farewell to their daughter as she leaves for her marital home.

Datar

The bride and the groom have to exchange salt three times without dropping any after the bride sprinkles milk in all the four corners of the house.

Sataurah

The newly wedded couple visits the bride's house post-wedding.
